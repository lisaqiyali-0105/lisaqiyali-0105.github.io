About Me

History major. Concert pianist. Morgan Stanley investment banker. An MBA who somehow wandered through the Boston Ballet, Christie's, and now — AI product manager. The thread through all of it? I'm a generalist who never fit neatly in a box, and I've stopped trying.

Building things is the latest expression of that. I used to assume "building things" was a credential you needed before you were allowed to start. Turns out in the age of AI, it's the other way around. I learn by showing up, and my generalist edge is knowing which rooms to walk into next. 🤖

Read → OpenAI · Feb 2026 Harness Engineering: Leveraging Codex in an Agent-First World

OpenAI's team built a real product with 0 lines of code written by a human. The lesson isn't about coding. When execution gets automated, every job shifts the same way: you stop being the one who does the work. You become the one who designs the environment where good work happens. Judgment, taste, and the ability to define what "right" looks like. That's the new bottleneck.

Read → Anthropic · recently Product Management on the AI Exponential

Anthropic's take on what PM actually means when the rate of change is non-linear. The part about taste and judgment becoming the scarce resource — not execution — landed. Everyone can ship faster now. The question is who's deciding what's worth shipping.

The piece I keep sending people when they ask why domain expertise still compounds. Everyone has access to the same models now — the edge is what sits on top. Judgment, pattern recognition, years of private reps that no one can access.
